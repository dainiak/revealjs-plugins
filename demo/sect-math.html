Math in Reveal.js

Below there are some slides showcasing math typesetting within slides.

Note that you can have native reveal.js fragments inside math formulas (for both KaTeX- and MathJax-powered typeset) and even inside SVGs (for MathJax-powered math).

A field \( \bbF \) with operations \( + \) and \( \cdot \).
  • commutative and associative,
  • distributivity of \( \cdot \) over \( + \),
  • existance of \( 0 \) and \( 1 \) such that \( 0+a=a \) for all \( a\in\bbF \) and \( 1\cdot b=b \) for all \( b\in\bbF\setminus\{0\} \).
  • equation \( a+x=b \) has a solution for all \( a, b\in\bbF \),
  • equation \( a\cdot x=b \) has a solution for all \( a\in\bbF\setminus\{0\} \) and \( b\in\bbF \).
Let \(\gamma\) be a primitive root modulo \(p\). Then

\(\exists t: \quad (\gamma+pt)^{p-1} = 1 + pu\), and \(p\notdivides u\).

\(\gamma + pt\) is a p.r. modulo \(p^\ell\) for every \(\ell\).

Let \(q:=(\gamma^{p-1} - 1)/p \in\bbZ\) (F. Little Th.), and let \(t=1+\gamma q\).

We have \[ \begin{array}{rl} (\gamma+pt)^{p-1} &\step{= \gamma^{p-1} + (p-1)\cdot \gamma^{p-2}\cdot pt + p^2\cdot \any} \\ &\step{= 1 + p\cdot (q-\gamma^{p-2}\cdot t + p\cdot \any)}\\ &\step{= 1 + p\cdot (q-\gamma^{p-2} - \gamma^{p-1}\cdot q + p\cdot \any)}\\ &\next{= 1 + p\cdot (-\gamma^{p-2} + p\cdot \any)} \end{array} \]

\[ (\gamma+pt)^{p-1} = 1 + pu,\text{ where } p\notdivides u. \] Then \[ \begin{array}{rl} (\gamma+pt)^{p\cdot(p-1)} & = (1 + pu)^p = 1 + p^2u + p^4\cdot\any\\ & \next{= 1 + p^2 u_1, \text{ where } p\notdivides u_1. } \end{array} \]

Similarly for every \(k\in\bbN\) we have \[ (\gamma+pt)^{p^k\cdot(p-1)} = 1 + p^{k+1} u_k, \text{ where } p\notdivides u_k. \]

If we fix a primitive root \(\alpha,\) then for every \(x\in \bbF\setminus\{0\}\) we can define a discrete logarithm: \(\log_\alpha x \), having many properties similar to the “ordinary logarithm”:

  • \(\log_\alpha (xy)=(\log_\alpha x + \log_\alpha y)\vstep{?}\next{\bmod \card\Fstar,}\)
  • \(x^{\log_\alpha y}=y^{\log_\alpha x},\)
  • \(\log_\alpha x = (\log_\beta x \cdot \log_\alpha \beta) \bmod\card\Fstar.\)
